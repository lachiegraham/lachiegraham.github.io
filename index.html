GREGS

a short film competition

You are hereby invited to participate in the GREGS film festival, by creating a short film with a 2-5 minute recommended length (and a maximum length of 10 minutes) to be shown at the GREGS award ceremony and movie premiere.

Participants will be provided with a set of guidelines for their films, and a period of two weeks to create their masterpieces.

Filmmakers and actors may produce/appear in multiple entries within the filming period to be viewed at a black tie movie screening event.

FAQ

The themes will be announced on February 15th.

Films are due February 28th at 11:59pm.

This gives you two weeks to do all script writing, filming, and editing for your film. Use the time wisely :)


  • Teams are decided by you
  • Teams can be of any size
  • You may submit multiple entries
  • Film can be any genre, so long as it is suitably short-film-like and fits the themes provided
  • Films must not exceed 10 mins in length
  • We recommend a length of roughly 3-5 mins

    • We don't have marking criteria, instead we have a bunch of awards that can be won. Here are a few of the award categories:

  • Best film
  • Most creative use of the themes
  • Best sound design
  • Funniest joke
  • Best costume design
  • Most interesting character
  • Best actor
  • Worst actor
  • ...and more!

    • Trophies will be supplied to winners of the various categories of awards, with judging being carried out by all participants during the GREGS award ceremony (everyone gets to vote).

    Note that the actual awards given out on the night will depend on what sort of films are submitted. E.g. if no comedy films are submitted, the 'best joke' award won't be used.

    Good news! We have a prize for the best foreign film (i.e. film not made in Canberra) so you can still participate!

    So find some people to make a film with, or do it by yourself. Good luck :)

    This whole thing is just for fun, so it's ok if you don't make something worthy of Sundance. Just make something a little bit silly and have fun with it!

    If time is really a concern for you, we suggest you pick a role that won't be too time consuming.
    For example, just being an actor probably won't take more than a few hours out of your day.
    On the flip side, editing can be a long and tedious task, so only volunteer for this if you're schedule is sufficiently empty :)
